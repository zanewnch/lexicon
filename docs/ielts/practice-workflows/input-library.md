# Input Library Workflow

## Purpose

Input Library is for building general English listening ability.

The goal is to make English sound familiar, increase vocabulary exposure, and improve comfort with different accents and topics.

This mode is not focused on IELTS scoring.

## Best Materials

Use interesting real-world English:

- Podcasts
- YouTube videos
- VoiceTube clips
- BBC-style news or documentaries
- Interviews
- University talks
- Short educational videos
- Daily conversation channels

Good IELTS-related themes:

- Education
- Environment
- Health
- Technology
- Culture
- Work
- Travel
- Science
- Society

## When To Practice

Recommended frequency:

- 10-30 minutes daily.
- During walking, commuting, chores, or low-pressure study time.
- On days when full IELTS practice feels too heavy.

Use this mode when:

- You want more English exposure.
- You feel tired but still want to do something useful.
- You need to get used to accents.
- You want to build vocabulary before doing harder IELTS material.

## Setup Before Starting

Prepare:

- One audio/video source
- Headphones
- Optional transcript or subtitles
- Listening log

Rules:

- Do not pause every sentence.
- Do not translate everything into Chinese.
- Do not treat it like a test.
- Focus on staying with the speaker.

## Full Practice Workflow

### Step 1: Choose One Topic

Pick a topic that is useful or interesting.

Examples:

- Climate change
- University life
- Public transport
- Food and health
- Psychology
- Technology
- Work culture

Topic choice matters because IELTS listening often uses academic and social topics.

### Step 2: First Listen For Gist

Listen continuously for 5-10 minutes.

Only answer:

- What is the main topic?
- What are the speaker's main points?
- Is the tone positive, negative, neutral, or mixed?
- What examples are mentioned?

Do not stop for unknown words unless they block the main idea.

### Step 3: Capture Useful Language

After listening, write down:

- 3 useful words
- 3 useful phrases
- 1 sentence pattern
- 1 topic idea

Example:

```text
Topic: urban transport
Words: congestion, commute, infrastructure
Phrases: reduce traffic pressure, public transport network, peak hours
Sentence pattern: One possible solution is to...
Topic idea: Cities can reduce car use by improving bus reliability.
```

### Step 4: Optional Second Listen

Listen again only if the material is useful.

Second listen focus:

- Catch more details
- Notice pronunciation
- Notice linking
- Notice how examples are introduced
- Notice useful IELTS vocabulary

Do not over-study every casual video. Save deep work for high-value materials.

### Step 5: Summarize In English

Write or say a short English summary.

Simple version:

```text
This episode is about...
The speaker explains that...
One example is...
I learned that...
```

Better version:

```text
The speaker argues that...
The main reason is...
This is supported by...
However, the speaker also mentions...
```

This connects listening with speaking and writing.

### Step 6: Add To Listening Log

Record:

- Source
- Topic
- Duration
- Difficulty
- Accent
- Useful vocabulary
- Whether to revisit

## Completion Standard

An Input Library session is complete when:

- At least 10 minutes of English audio is heard.
- The main idea is understood.
- A short log is written.
- At least 3 useful words or phrases are saved.

Input Library is not complete if the audio only plays in the background and nothing is remembered.
