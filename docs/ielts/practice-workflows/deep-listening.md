# Deep Listening Workflow

## Purpose

Deep Listening is for finding the exact reason you failed to understand something.

The goal is not speed. The goal is diagnosis: sound, vocabulary, spelling, grammar, paraphrase, or attention.

## Best Materials

Use materials with audio and transcript:

- Official IELTS listening audio
- Cambridge IELTS listening audio
- British Council / IDP sample tests
- IELTS Bro / 雅思哥 listening practice used externally
- Any reliable audio with transcript

Deep Listening works best after a Mock Test because you already know which parts caused mistakes.

## When To Practice

Use Deep Listening when:

- You got many answers wrong.
- You heard the audio but could not catch the answer.
- You often understand after reading the transcript.
- You are weak at connected speech.
- You want to improve accuracy, not just do more tests.

Recommended frequency:

- 15-30 minutes per day.
- 3-5 wrong-answer clips after each mock test.
- One difficult IELTS section at a time, not a whole test.

## Setup Before Starting

Prepare:

- Audio player with rewind controls
- Transcript hidden at first
- Text editor or notebook
- Mistake log
- Optional playback speed control

Rules:

- Start with normal speed.
- Do not look at the transcript before trying.
- Work sentence by sentence or short phrase by short phrase.
- Stop before fatigue makes listening careless.

## Full Practice Workflow

### Step 1: Select A Short Segment

Choose a difficult segment.

Good segment length:

- 10-30 seconds for beginners
- 30-60 seconds for intermediate practice
- One full paragraph only after skill improves

Pick segments from:

- Questions you got wrong
- Fast speaker turns
- Part 3 discussion
- Part 4 lecture
- Map direction descriptions
- Number/address/name sections

### Step 2: First Listen Without Writing

Play the segment once.

Only answer:

- What is the general meaning?
- Who is speaking?
- What is the situation?
- Where might the answer be?

Do not pause yet.

### Step 3: Listen And Transcribe

Play one sentence or phrase.

Type exactly what you hear.

Use blanks for missing words:

```text
The course will begin on ____ and the fee is ____.
```

Replay up to three times.

Rules:

- First replay: catch content words.
- Second replay: catch grammar words.
- Third replay: check spelling and endings.

After three tries, stop guessing and move to transcript comparison.

### Step 4: Compare With Transcript

Reveal the transcript and compare.

Mark differences:

- Missing words
- Wrong words
- Wrong endings
- Wrong spelling
- Misheard numbers
- Missed linking
- Missed weak forms

Example:

```text
Heard: the fee is fifteen pounds
Transcript: the fee is fifty pounds
Mistake: number contrast / vowel clarity
```

### Step 5: Label The Mistake

Choose one or more labels:

- Linking
- Weak form
- Reduction
- Accent
- Unknown vocabulary
- Known word but not recognized in speech
- Number
- Date
- Name
- Spelling
- Singular/plural
- Paraphrase
- Distractor
- Lost focus

The label is important because it decides the next drill.

### Step 6: Shadow The Sentence

Play the sentence again and speak with the audio.

Do this 3-5 times:

1. Listen and read transcript.
2. Speak with the audio.
3. Speak without audio.
4. Replay and compare rhythm.

Focus on:

- Stress
- Pauses
- Linking
- Weak words
- Final consonants

### Step 7: Relisten Without Transcript

Hide the transcript.

Play the same segment again.

Check:

- Can you now hear the missing words?
- Can you hear the answer location?
- Can you recognize the same sound pattern?

If yes, mark the segment as learned.

If no, save it for another day.

## Completion Standard

A Deep Listening session is complete when:

- At least 5-10 difficult sentences are processed, or 15-30 minutes are completed.
- Each missed sentence has a mistake label.
- At least 3 sentences are shadowed.
- One weak point is added to the Targeted Drill list.

Deep Listening is not complete if you only read the transcript passively.
